Capital Gains Tax 2025: Short-Term vs. Long-Term

Published: December 16, 2025 | Category: Investing Tax

Golden Rule: How long you hold an asset determines how much tax you pay. Holding for more than 1 year can save you a lot of money!

1. Short-Term Capital Gains

If you sell an asset (like stocks or crypto) that you held for one year or less, the profit is taxed as "ordinary income."

This means it is taxed at the same rate as your salary (up to 37%). This is why short-term trading is expensive regarding taxes.

2. Long-Term Capital Gains

If you hold the asset for more than one year before selling, you qualify for lower tax rates.

Depending on your total income, your Long-Term Capital Gains tax rate will be 0%, 15%, or 20%.

2025 Long-Term Tax Rates (Single Filers)

Your Taxable Income Your Tax Rate
$0 to $48,350 0% (Tax Free!)
$48,351 to $533,400 15%
Over $533,400 20%
