Federal Income Tax Brackets 2024-2025: A Complete Guide

Published: December 16, 2025 | Category: Tax Guide

Quick Summary: The US has a progressive tax system. This means as your income increases, you pay higher tax rates only on the money that falls into higher brackets, not on your total income.

What are Tax Brackets?

Federal income tax rates are divided into seven sections, known as brackets. For the 2024 and 2025 tax years, these rates are: 10%, 12%, 22%, 24%, 32%, 35%, and 37%.

Many people misunderstand how brackets work. Being in the "22% bracket" doesn't mean you pay 22% on all your money. You only pay 22% on the portion of income that exceeds the limit of the 12% bracket.

2025 Tax Brackets (For Taxes Due April 2026)

The IRS adjusts these numbers annually for inflation. Here is the breakdown for Single Filers:

Tax Rate Taxable Income (Single)
10% $0 to $11,925
12% $11,926 to $48,475
22% $48,476 to $103,350
24% $103,351 to $197,300

Standard Deduction

Before calculating your tax, don't forget to subtract the Standard Deduction. For 2025, the standard deduction for single filers has increased to $15,000.
