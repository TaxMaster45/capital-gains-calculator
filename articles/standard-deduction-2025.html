Standard Deduction vs. Itemized: Maximizing Your 2025 Refund

Choosing between the standard deduction and itemizing can significantly impact your tax bill. For the 2025 tax season, the IRS has increased standard deduction amounts to account for inflation. Let's find out which path gets you more money back.

2025 Standard Deduction Amounts

Filing Status 2025 Deduction Amount
Single / Married Filing Separately $15,000
Married Filing Jointly $30,000
Head of Household $22,500

When Should You Itemize?

You should consider itemizing your deductions (using Schedule A) if your total deductible expenses exceed the standard deduction amount for your status. Common deductible expenses include:

  • Mortgage interest on up to $750,000 of debt.
  • State and Local Taxes (SALT) up to $10,000.
  • Unreimbursed medical expenses exceeding 7.5% of your AGI.
  • Charitable contributions.

The Verdict

For nearly 90% of US taxpayers, the Standard Deduction is the better and easier option. However, if you are a homeowner in a high-tax state or had major medical bills this year, itemizing could save you thousands.
